There's a version of the "small acts, big impact" idea that I find unhelpful — the one that says individual choices matter more than systemic change. That is not what I mean here.

What I mean is something more local: that the quality of your attention on small things shapes the quality of your attention on large things. You don't get to be careless about the small things and then suddenly careful when something important arrives. Attention is a practice, not a setting you switch.

This matters most to me in work. The note you take carefully even when you're tired. The question you ask when you already think you know the answer. The habit of looking again before you conclude.

None of these produce results directly. They produce a person who is more likely to notice something worth noticing. The results, if they come, come from that.
